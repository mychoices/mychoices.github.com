Hello, it's me.

Programmer turned designer

I'm a self-taught designer, but don't let my lack of formal design education fool you. Being an autodidact has shaped me to be proactive, curious, and humble. I've also had exposure to wearing multiple hats, from product management to customer support. I'm currently working as a part-time freelancer while traveling the U.S.

Designing for others

My pivot into design occurred after experiencing multiple failures in the startup space. Time and time again, I found my colleagues and I didn't spend enough time validating whether we were building the right product for the right people. I turned to design thinking to adopt a philosphy that emphasizes an empathetic and creative approach to solving problems.

Always learning

When I'm not designing, you can find me trying to make sense of the world and myself through photography, journaling, and traveling. I seek out experiences that push me to grow. I mentioned I'm traveling the U.S., right? I forgot to say. I'm doing this while living out of a minivan.
