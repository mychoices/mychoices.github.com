Gritcube provides African entrepreneurs with the resources to launch and grow.

Description:

Gritcube is a non-profit that focuses on connecting African entrepreneurs with volunteer consultants. I worked closely with their Marketing Coordinator and President to redevelop their marketing website.

Involvement:

UX Design, Branding, & Front-End Web Development

How might we improve Gritcube‚Äôs online presence?

Initially, the reasons for undergoing the redesign were vague. Gritcube wanted the website to be more professional and concise. After three remote interviews, I found more specific pain points. The call-to-actions were unclear, and the content was difficult to skim.

Difficult to skim

Lengthy paragraphs and over-usage of centered text deterred interviewees from wanting to read sections such as the team‚Äôs background information.

Unclear call-to-actions

Each page either lacked a call-to-action or the call-to-action didn‚Äôt stand out from the rest of the page‚Äôs content.

Restructuring page layouts

In total, we reduced the copy by over 70%. Referencing the interviews, I deleted or moved sections to a different page. For example, I removed the paragraphs of the startups and consultants on the homepage because interviewees ignored them. Instead, interviewees visited the corresponding pages when they wanted information on consultants or startups. I also emphasized sections that proved helpful such as the ‚Äúhow it works‚Äù diagram.

Refreshing the brand

I found there was a disconnect between Gritcube's ideal and existing brand identity. By examining non-profits Gritcube admired, such as Charity Water and Watsi, I decided to expand the color palette by experimenting with the opacity and saturation of the logo's colors. I also switched the typeface to Montserrat for its inherent contrast influenced by lettering in urban spaces.

Project 2 of 2

FreshPool offers affordable, on-demand service for swimming pools.

Description:

FreshPool is a traditional pool service company that wants to use technology to pivot to an on-demand business model. I worked closely with Carey Janecka and the founders to design mobile apps for pool owners and technicians.

Involvement:

Product Design & Branding

FreshPool is a fictional name for confidential purposes.

How might FreshPool become a technology company?

After in-depth interviews with the founders and two pool technicians. I created user flows to explore the experience of the mobile apps. I trusted the founders' expertise to understand how people signed up for a pool service, and I talked with the pool technicians to learn about their workday.

Iterating with usability studies

We initially assumed FreshPool should allow customers to select pool technicians, and we were unsure if signing up for a service should use step-by-step screens or a list view. I interviewed four potential customers, and I found they didn‚Äôt want to wait for technicians to accept their request. In a separate study, Carey learned people found it easier to follow step-by-step screens.

Emphasizing simple

FreshPool wanted a minimal visual language, so we opted for a predominantly muted color palette and a single modern typeface. After creating the high-fidelity mockups, I used an InVision prototype and freehand to hand off the designs.
