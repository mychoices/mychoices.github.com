Gritcube provides African entrepreneurs with the resources to launch and grow.

Description:

Gritcube is a non-profit that focuses on connecting African entrepreneurs with volunteer consultants. I worked closely with their Marketing Coordinator and President to redevelop their marketing website.

Involvement:

UX Design, Branding, & Front-End Web Development

How might we improve Gritcube‚Äôs online presence?

Initially, the reasons for undergoing the redesign were vague: Gritcube thought the website needed to be more professional and clear. After three remote interviews, we found the website already met those qualities, but the interviews revealed other pain points: the call-to-actions were unclear and the content was difficult to skim.

Lack of skimmability

Lengthy paragraphs and over-usage of centered text deterred interviewees from wanting to read sections such as the team‚Äôs background information.

Unclear call-to-actions

Each page either lacked a call-to-action or the call-to-action didn‚Äôt stand out from the rest of the page‚Äôs content.

Page layouts reorganized

Based on the interviews, I deleted unnecessary sections or moved them to different pages. In the case of the homepage, I removed the paragraphs of the startups and consultants. I found interviewees ignored the paragraphs, and instead, they visited the pages when they wanted information on either group. I also emphasized sections the interviewees found helpful such as the ‚Äúhow it works‚Äù diagram. In total, we reduced the content by 50%.

Responsive with personality

FreshPool offers affordable, on-demand service for swimming pools.

Description:

FreshPool, which is a fictional name for confidentiality purposes, is a traditional pool service company that wants to use technology to pivot to an on-demand business model. I worked closely with the founders and Carey Janecka to design the beta versions of their two mobile apps for customers and pool technicians.

Involvement:

Product Design, & Branding

How might we help FreshPool adopt an on-demand service?

We kicked off the projects with in-depth interviews. I trusted FreshPool‚Äôs expertise to understand how people signed up for a pool service, and I talked with two pool technicians to understand their typical workday. I then used flowcharts to explore the experience of the mobile apps.

Wireframe iterations with usability studies

We initially assumed FreshPool should allow customers to select pool technicians, and we were unsure if signing up for a service should use step-by-step screens or a list view. I interviewed four potential customers, and I found they didn‚Äôt want to have to wait for technicians to accept a service request. On a separate study, Carey learned the step-by-step flow was easier to follow.

Emphasis on simple

FreshPool wanted a minimal visual language, so we opted for a predominantly muted color palette and a single modern typeface. After creating the high-fidelity mockups, I used an InVision prototype and freehand to hand off the designs.
